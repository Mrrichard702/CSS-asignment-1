Indigenous People of Treaty 5

The first time people met about the treaty was on the 20th of september, when Alexander Morris and James Mckay met with Berens River bands at the Wesleyan Mission School House. There, they secured the terms of the treaty with little hinderence except for a small discussion on the terms for election of the chiefs. The commissioners would eventually discover that this would be brought up again later with subsequent treaty discussions for the next two years. The next day the commissioners moved north following the Nelson River. On the 24th of september they would meet up with two groups of Cree: Christian converts and non-Christians both a part of the Wood band. after talks and translations of the terms of the treaty it was signed. Morris and Mckay informed those that wanted to move south to farm at Fisher River could as a reserve would be constructed there. while many christian bands opted in to relocate those of the Wood band chose to stay behind at their home in Cross Lake, they would agree upon another reserve be made.
