Indigenous People of Treaty 5's Adhesion

The Goverment found new use for the northern Canadian region because of oil in the Mackenzie River area. The Goverment purposefully cut the benefits of Treaties as instead of making it a part of Treaty 10 which had better benefits then Treaty 5.In 1907 the First Nations People occupying the land of what would be The adhesion to Treaty 5 where the Cree bands living in the Nelson House and Split Lake. Later in 1908 the Indian Agent Reverend John Semmens Continued his objective of adding more First Nations People to Treaty 5. He was able to get signatures from 350 from mainly Cree bands such as Cross Lake, Fisher River, and Norway House. Some information about the adhesion's to certain Treaties was that those who weren't included in the previous version of the treaty would be joining that prior version of the treaty. This Treaty is being somewhat used as there isn't an organization that represents all of Treaty 5 but Nishnawbe Aski Nation (NAN) represents portions of Treaty 5 and Treaty 9. NAN's goal is to protect the signatories and protect the Indigenous rights, Promote Indigenous business and culture, and improve the quality of education and life in Nishnawbe Aski territory.
