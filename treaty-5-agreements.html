The deal made between Indigenous Peoples and Europeans

Treaty 5 was signed on the 2nd of july 1875 and the Treaty 5 adhesion was signed on the 8th of july 1908. the Indigenous People that signed Treaty 5 were the Ojibwa and the Swampy Cree. The agreements made within the treaty was that the Indigenous People would surrender all land in exchange for reserve land, the right to hunt, trap, and fish. some differences between Treaty 5 and other Treaties was that they receive only a quarter of land per family comparded to Treaties 3 and 4. As well as receiving a 5 dollar up front payment.
